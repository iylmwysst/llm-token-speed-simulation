About

About Pipat Khambun

I am an AI researcher and systems designer focused on developer tools, interface systems, and practical AI experiences that people can actually feel, not just benchmark.

Why this site exists

LLM Token Speed Simulation was built to make model speed easier to reason about from a product perspective. Teams often discuss tokens per second as an infrastructure metric, but the user experiences pacing, readability, and waiting. This project turns that gap into something visible.

My goal with the site is simple: help product teams, designers, and engineers evaluate streaming UX in a more concrete way, especially when language, reading rhythm, and answer density all change how “fast” a response actually feels.

Background

My work sits at the intersection of AI research, software systems, and interface design. I am also the founder of CodeWebway, where I focus on tools that make web-based development workflows feel more direct and seamless.

In 2025, I co-authored research on a low-cost smart cane for visually impaired users, contributing most of the implementation and part of the system design. The project combined computer vision, sensing, and assistive interaction, and reflects the kind of work I care about most: systems that are technically grounded and useful in practice.

AI UX

I care about how AI feels in use, especially around pacing, readability, and interaction quality rather than raw model claims alone.

Systems design
